Lounès Matoub

His rebellious song still carries on

Here's a time line of Lounès Matoub's life:

  • 1956 - Born in the village of Taourirt Moussa in Algerian Kabylia.
  • 1968 - the Algerian government introduced a policy of Arabization in the education system. Matoub reacted by skipping school.
  • 1975 - He had abandoned formal education. He left for France in search of work.
  • 1978 - He recorded his first album Ay Izem (The Lion)
  • 1980 - He gave his first major concert at the time of the "Berber Spring" protest movement in Kabylia.
  • 1988 - Matoub was shot five times by a policeman and left for dead.
  • 1992 - The Islamist Armed Islamic Group (GIA) added his name to a hitlist of artists and intellectuals. Matoub remained in Algeria.
  • 1994 - Matoub received Le Prix de la Mémoire ("The Memorial Prize") from Mrs. Danielle Mitterrand, President of La Fondation France Libertés ("The French Liberties Foundation") in Paris.
  • 1998 - Matoub's car was stopped at a roadblock while he was driving along a mountainous road in eastern Algeria (Kabylia). The car was fired upon by masked gunmen, killing Matoub.
